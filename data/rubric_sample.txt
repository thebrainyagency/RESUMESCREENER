Projects / Initiatives (30 points)
25–30: Multiple self-initiated GenAI projects (chatbots, LangChain workflows, vector DB experiments, hackathons, GitHub repos with demos). Shows initiative and depth.
15–20: 2–3 strong course or guided projects with some originality.
5–10: Tutorial-level work, academic exercises only.
0–4: Minimal evidence of hands-on work.
Focus: Has the person actually tinkered? Can they show something they built, even if small?

Technical Skills (25 points)
20–25: Comfortable with Python, prompt engineering, LangChain/LangGraph, embeddings, vector DBs (Pinecone, Chroma, FAISS), HuggingFace. Some cloud or infra is a plus.
15–19: Good Python plus some GenAI exposure (basic LangChain, RAG, chatbot builds).
8–14: Strong coding but limited GenAI frameworks.
0–7: Weak coding foundation or no GenAI exposure.
Focus: Can they code and use modern GenAI tools, or will they need to learn from scratch?

Relevant Exposure (15 points)
12–15: Hackathons, research labs, internships, or open-source in AI/automation.
8–11: College courses plus some side projects.
3–7: Coursework only, no real-world application.
0–2: No exposure.
Focus: Have they worked in environments where creativity and collaboration mattered?

Learning Agility and Curiosity (10 points)
9–10: Actively blogs, shares demos, experiments with cutting-edge tools (LangGraph, MCP, AutoGen). Obsessed with trying new frameworks.
6–8: Reads and experiments regularly, can explain what they have learned.
3–5: Some curiosity, limited evidence of exploration.
0–2: Passive learner, no signs of self-driven exploration.
Focus: Do they have the tinkerer mindset to keep up in a fast-moving field?

Communication (10 points)
9–10: Excellent clarity in explaining projects (well-documented GitHub, blogs, videos).
6–8: Clear resume, can describe work well.
3–5: Average communication, limited project documentation.
0–2: Poor clarity, unclear resume or project descriptions.
Focus: Can they communicate ideas to teammates and future clients?

Innovation and Collaboration (5 points)
5: Hackathon wins, creative or unusual projects, strong teamwork.
3–4: Some evidence of creativity or group projects.
1–2: Limited innovation.
0: None.
Focus: Are they creative problem-solvers who can work in a team?

Education and Academic Rigor (5 points)
5: Top-tier college, AI/ML electives, or strong online certifications (DeepLearning.ai, HuggingFace, fast.ai).
3–4: Decent college plus relevant coursework.
1–2: General degree, minimal AI coursework.
0: No relevant coursework.
Focus: Do they have enough foundational grounding to grow fast?
